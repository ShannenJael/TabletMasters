## 1. Purpose

The Parties wish to evaluate and discuss a possible business relationship involving the sale, distribution, servicing, support, fulfillment, procurement, marketing, financing, or other commercial arrangements related to tablets, accessories, managed services, warranty programs, insurance offerings, logistics, and related business opportunities (the "Purpose"). In connection with the Purpose, each Party may disclose Confidential Information to the other Party.

## 2. Definition of Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party ("Disclosing Party") to the other Party ("Receiving Party"), whether disclosed orally, visually, electronically, in writing, or by inspection, that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes:

1. business plans, pricing, forecasts, margins, revenue models, and financial information;
2. customer, supplier, reseller, and partner information;
3. product roadmaps, specifications, sourcing information, repair processes, service methods, and operational procedures;
4. software, systems, integrations, technical documentation, and security-related information;
5. marketing plans, sales strategies, market analyses, and non-public commercial terms;
6. notes, summaries, analyses, and other materials derived from the foregoing.

Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate by written records:

1. was publicly available at the time of disclosure or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement;
2. was already lawfully known to the Receiving Party without confidentiality restriction before disclosure by the Disclosing Party;
3. is lawfully received from a third party without breach of any confidentiality obligation; or
4. is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information.

## 3. Use and Protection of Confidential Information

The Receiving Party shall:

1. use the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information solely for the Purpose;
2. protect the Confidential Information using at least reasonable care, and no less than the care it uses to protect its own similar confidential information;
3. limit access to employees, officers, directors, advisors, affiliates, contractors, financing sources, and representatives who have a need to know the information for the Purpose and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those in this Agreement;
4. not disclose the Confidential Information to any other person without the Disclosing Party's prior written consent; and
5. be responsible for any breach of this Agreement by its representatives.

## 4. Required Disclosure

If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, court order, or governmental process to disclose any Confidential Information, it may do so only to the extent legally required, provided that, unless prohibited by law, the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice and reasonable cooperation, at the Disclosing Party's expense, to seek confidential treatment, a protective order, or other appropriate remedy.

## 5. No License; No Obligation

All Confidential Information remains the property of the Disclosing Party. No license or other rights under any patent, copyright, trademark, trade secret, or other intellectual property right are granted or implied by this Agreement except the limited right to use Confidential Information for the Purpose. Neither Party is obligated under this Agreement to proceed with any transaction or relationship.

## 6. Return or Destruction

Upon written request of the Disclosing Party, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information, except that the Receiving Party may retain:

1. copies maintained in routine backup systems that are not readily accessible in the ordinary course, provided they remain subject to this Agreement; and
2. copies required to be retained by law, regulation, compliance policy, or bona fide internal recordkeeping requirements, provided they remain subject to this Agreement.

Upon request, an authorized representative of the Receiving Party shall certify completion of the return or destruction requirement.

## 7. Term and Survival

This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues for two (2) years unless earlier terminated by either Party upon thirty (30) days' written notice. Confidential Information disclosed during the term of this Agreement shall remain subject to this Agreement for five (5) years from the date of disclosure, except that trade secrets shall be protected for as long as they remain trade secrets under applicable law.

## 8. Remedies

The Parties acknowledge that unauthorized use or disclosure of Confidential Information may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages alone may be inadequate. Accordingly, the Disclosing Party may seek injunctive relief or other equitable remedies, in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity.

## 9. No Warranty

All Confidential Information is provided "as is." Neither Party makes any representation or warranty, express or implied, regarding the accuracy or completeness of any Confidential Information, except as may be set forth in a later definitive written agreement signed by both Parties.
