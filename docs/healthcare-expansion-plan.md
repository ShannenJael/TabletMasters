# Healthcare Expansion Plan

## Goal
Build a healthcare service line for Tablet Masters focused first on hospital and clinic tablet operations, then later on patient-facing workflows, while staying disciplined about HIPAA and PHI boundaries.

## Public Positioning
The public-facing offer should focus on:
- hospital tablet fleets
- clinic and care-facility tablet deployments
- kiosk and intake devices
- patient education tablets
- shared-device support
- repair, replacement, and redeployment

The public-facing offer should avoid implying:
- EHR integration
- clinical decision support
- regulated medical software
- protected health information handling unless the business is actually ready for it

## Why This Matters
Hospitals and care settings often need tablets for:
- patient intake
- bedside education
- check-in and registration
- internal operational workflows
- shared-use device programs
- controlled kiosk environments

Tablet Masters can credibly support that operational layer now.

## Compliance Boundary
Official HHS guidance makes the boundary clear:
- A business associate is an entity performing services involving PHI on behalf of a covered entity.
- Covered entities generally need written business associate agreements with business associates.
- HIPAA risk increases materially if Tablet Masters creates, receives, maintains, or transmits protected health information on behalf of hospitals or clinics.

Reference links:
- https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/privacy/guidance/business-associates/index.html
- https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/privacy/guidance/standards-privacy-individually-identifiable-health-information/index.html
- https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/covered-entities/sample-business-associate-agreement-provisions

This is an operational planning note, not legal advice.

## Recommended Market Entry
### Phase 1: Operational tablet support
Market:
- hospitals
- outpatient clinics
- urgent care groups
- rehab centers
- long-term care and assisted living
- specialty practices

Offer:
- tablet procurement
- fleet setup
- kiosk mode and locked-down configurations
- patient education devices
- shared-device accessory planning
- repair and redeployment support

### Phase 2: Patient experience workflows
Expand into:
- bedside education
- discharge content delivery
- communication and interpreter support devices
- patient-facing waiting-room devices

This phase still avoids direct PHI-heavy service claims unless legal and technical readiness is in place.

### Phase 3: PHI-sensitive services only if ready
Only consider this phase after:
- legal review
- documented security controls
- breach response process
- access control process
- audit logging strategy
- BAA readiness

Potential later services:
- workflow support involving PHI
- integrations tied to healthcare systems
- managed environments with higher compliance requirements

## Use Cases to Emphasize
- intake and registration tablets
- patient education tablets
- discharge instruction devices
- kiosk-mode devices for controlled workflows
- shared-use devices in operational departments
- staff support tablets

## Use Cases to Avoid Marketing Too Early
- charting
- EHR workflows
- PHI transmission
- anything that sounds like medical software or clinical platform development

## Risks
- overclaiming healthcare compliance
- unintentionally stepping into PHI-handling territory
- using language that implies medical certification
- attracting opportunities that require security maturity the business does not yet have

## Internal Readiness Checklist
Before moving beyond the public operational offer, answer:
- Will Tablet Masters ever store PHI?
- Will Tablet Masters ever access PHI while supporting hospital clients?
- Will hospitals require BAAs?
- What security controls exist today?
- What breach response process exists today?
- Is there a documented device wipe and redeployment process?
- Is there staff training for healthcare environments?

- prepare internal security and contract readiness before marketing anything more aggressive
